Real cognitive science research does not need a lab. It needs a laptop, and someone to show you how.

bioIDE Research publishes original computational neuroscience and cognitive science research from high school researchers, and helps students with an idea but no lab figure out where to start.

Submit Your Research Join As A Reviewer
The Problem

Most students who want to study the brain hit the same wall.

Most high school students who want to do real cognitive science or neuroscience research hit the same wall: no lab, no funding, and no professor down the hall. So the idea never goes anywhere, even with the curiosity and the drive to do it.

Our Solution

Real, publishable cognitive science research does not actually require a lab. Free computational tools can produce real findings using nothing but a laptop. Most students simply do not know this path exists. bioIDE Research exists to show them.

What We Publish

Three formats, one rule: it has to be computational, not bench work.

01

Original Research

Docking studies, ADMET/SwissADME analyses, network or data-driven cognitive science work: anything run on a laptop, not in a wet lab.

02

Literature Reviews

A lower barrier to entry for students newer to research: synthesize and critically engage with existing findings on a cognitive science question.

03

Methods & Tools

Short, practical explainers on a free tool or technique, written by students for students who do not yet know where to start.

How It Works

From submission to publication.

1

Submit

Open call, rolling submissions. Original research or a literature review, your choice.

2

Peer Review

Two student reviewers read your work and give feedback using a clear rubric.

3

Advisor Check

A scientific advisor spot-checks methodology on original research before it moves forward.

4

Revise

You revise based on feedback. This is mentorship, not just gatekeeping.

5

Publish

Your work goes up on the site, free to read, no paywall, ever.

Join The Editorial Board

Become a Reviewer

Reviewers are the backbone of bioIDE Research. Here is exactly what the role involves before you apply.

What Reviewers Do

Read, evaluate, and help shape what gets published.

A

Review Submissions

Every paper gets two student reviewers. You will read submissions in your area of interest and give written feedback using our rubric.

B

Evaluate Methodology

Check that computational methods are sound and clearly explained, even if you are still learning yourself. Advisors back up your review on original research.

C

Time Commitment

Roughly two to four papers per month, depending on submission volume. You choose which submissions to take on.

D

Who Can Apply

Any high school student, anywhere in the US. Prior research experience is a plus but not required.

For Scientists And Educators

Join the Advisory Board

Lend expertise to a student-run journal making cognitive science research accessible to high schoolers without lab access.

What Advisors Do

A light, flexible commitment.

A

Spot-Check Methodology

Before original research is published, one advisor reviews the methods section to confirm the science is sound.

B

Mentor First-Time Researchers

Occasionally answer a methods question from a student author who is working through their first real research project.

C

Time Commitment

No fixed schedule. Advisors review on an as-needed basis, typically a short written check per accepted paper.

D

Who We Are Looking For

Graduate students, postdocs, faculty, or industry researchers in neuroscience, cognitive science, or computational biology.
